Mission Deep Dives

James Webb Space Telescope

Launched on December 25, 2021, the James Webb Space Telescope operates 1.5 million km from Earth. It observes the universe in infrared, allowing it to see through cosmic dust clouds and detect light from the universe's first galaxies — formed just 300 million years after the Big Bang. Its images have already rewritten textbooks on galaxy formation and stellar evolution.

Perseverance Rover — Mars 2020

NASA's Perseverance Rover landed on Mars in February 2021. It is the most sophisticated rover ever sent to another planet, equipped with 23 cameras, a drill for collecting rock samples, and the Ingenuity helicopter — the first powered aircraft to fly on another world. It is also producing oxygen from the Martian atmosphere.
