Space Facts and Glossary

Planet Rankings by Size

  1. Jupiter — Largest (142,984 km diameter)
  2. Saturn — Second largest, famous rings
  3. Uranus — Third largest ice giant
  4. Neptune — Windiest planet
  5. Earth — Only known life-bearing world
  6. Venus — Hottest surface (464°C)
  7. Mars — Humanity's next target
  8. Mercury — Smallest, fastest orbit

Amazing Space Facts

  • A day on Venus is longer than its year
  • Saturn would float in a giant bathtub
  • There are more stars than grains of sand on Earth
  • Light from the Sun takes 8 minutes to reach Earth
  • Space is completely silent — no medium for sound
  • Jupiter's Great Red Spot is slowly shrinking

Space Glossary

Nebula
A giant cloud of gas and dust where new stars are born.
Black Hole
A region where gravity is so strong not even light escapes.
Light Year
The distance light travels in one year — 9.46 trillion km.
Exoplanet
A planet orbiting a star outside our solar system.
